    Windows XP was released by Microsoft on October 25, 2001, and became one of the most popular operating systems. 
    Known for its iconic start menu and blue taskbar, XP introduced a more user-friendly interface and improved stability compared to its predecessors. 
    It featured notable enhancements like System Restore and Windows Movie Maker, transforming the computing experience. 
    Despite its widespread use, Microsoft ended support for Windows XP on April 8, 2014, urging users to upgrade to newer systems due to security concerns. 
    Its legacy continues to influence modern operating systems.
